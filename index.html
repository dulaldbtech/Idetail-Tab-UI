Insulin-independent mechanism

SGLT2 inhibition reduces glucose
reabsorption by the kidneys and
increases glucose excretion in the urine.

SGLT2 inhibitors act on a pathway
independent of the level of β cell function
to provide a glucose-lowering effect.

Core benefits
  • HbA1c reduction
  • Low risk of hypoglycaemia
    (unless used with an SU)
  • Well tolerated
Additional benefits
  • Weight loss*
  • Systolic BP reduction*
Insulin-dependent mechanism

DPP4 inhibition increases concentrations
of the incretin hormones GLP-1 and GIP,
which results in a glucose-dependent
increase in insulin secretion, reduction
in glucagon secretion, improved glucose
uptake in peripheral tissue and reduced
hepatic glucose production.

Core benefits
  • Additional HbA1c reduction
  • Low risk of hypoglycaemia
    (unless used with an SU)
  • Well tolerated
